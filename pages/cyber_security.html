    Lukaskrankenhaus Neuss

    Lukaskrankenhaus Neuss, a public hospital founded in 1911 in Neuss, Germany suffered from a ransomware attack in February 2016, initiated through a social engineering tactic. Error messages were sent to employees and an email address was given to contact, which they did not.

    While the servers and computer systems were taken offline, staff had to use pen, paper and fax machines to continue their work and high-risk procedures had to be postponed. Hours of data was lost and the handwritten records had to be integrated with the EHR again afterwards. This attack impacted the hospital for months before their workflow could be returned to normal.

    Source: Argaw ST, Troncoso-Pastoriza JR, Lacey D, Florin M-V, Calcavecchia F, Anderson D, et al. Cybersecurity of Hospitals: discussing the challenges and working towards mitigating the risks. BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making. 2020 Jul 3;20(1):146.
    South-eastern Norway regional health authority (South-East RHF)

    In January 2018, South-East RHF announced that the PHI and records of nearly 2.9 million people had been compromised. The organisation of specialist hospitals and healthcare services had begun acting to reduce the security risks from the legacy system Windows XP and had been planning to phase it out, the vulnerability was exploited before these security measures were implemented.

    The motivations were suspected to be targeting patient health data and the organisation's interaction with Norway's armed forces. There were no significant risks in patient safety and hospitals' workflow, but this warned of future attacks on health data and urged for GDPR.

    Source: Argaw ST, Troncoso-Pastoriza JR, Lacey D, Florin M-V, Calcavecchia F, Anderson D, et al. Cybersecurity of Hospitals: discussing the challenges and working towards mitigating the risks. BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making. 2020 Jul 3;20(1):146.

    The Importance of Cyber Security

    Financial losses are typically attributed to randomware attacks, during which critical medical processes are slowed or become completely inoperable. The workflow is slowed and funds which could have been allocated to the modernisation or the hospital are lost. These factors mean patient care becomes inaccessible.

    As this attack requires little to no technical skills, the failure to implement basic cyber security protocols such as up-to-date anti-virus software, proper email filtering and regular back-ups can cause devastating losses when threatened with randomware.

    Source: Cyber Attacks: In the Healthcare Sector [Internet]. CIS - Center for Internet Security.

    When access to medical records and important medical devices means the mission of hospitals to care effectively for patients becomes deterred greatly. Having confidential patient data stolen can lead to major risks if the data is changed.

    Source: Cyber Attacks: In the Healthcare Sector [Internet]. CIS - Center for Internet Security.
